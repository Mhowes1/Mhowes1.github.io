I pulled the key style numbers for Buzz’s teams and for Maryland this season, then boiled each group down into one clean summary line so I could compare them directly. For possessions, I used the standard estimate — FGA minus offensive rebounds plus turnovers plus 0.44 times free throws — so it accounts for second chances and free-throw trips.

Maryland looks a lot like a typical Buzz Williams team, but the gaps are pretty telling in a few places.

Maryland’s 69.39 possessions is almost identical to Buzz’s 68.86. Tempo-wise, they play the same game. Free throw emphasis: Maryland’s free-throw rate (0.37) is basically Buzz-level (0.39). Both offenses lean heavily on physical, whistle-generating drives.

Offensive rebounding: Maryland grabs 9.46 per game — right in Buzz’s wheelhouse (9.61). The effort level is similar.

Maryland falls short of several traits that have defined Buzz Williams’ teams. Buzz’s groups typically take more threes (39% to Maryland’s 36%) and get to the foul line at a slightly higher rate. They also handle the ball cleaner — Buzz teams post an 11.52 turnover rate, compared to Maryland’s 10.43.
