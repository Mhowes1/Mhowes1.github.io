Trouble in Laker Land? How Lebron and Luka’s pairing looks this season.

Author

Michael Howes

Published

December 2, 2025

LeBron James was supposed to share the floor with Luka Dončić and form one of the league’s most overwhelming duos.

Instead, the Lakers’ early-season data shows something nobody expected. LeBron’s most effective minutes — by far — are happening without Luka next to him. The lineups that pop are the ones filled with role players who let him run the show the old-fashioned way.

And until the two stars figure out how to share the wheel, the Lakers look more like two separate teams than a supercharged contender.

The Lakers’ best lineups so far are Luka-led groups without LeBron, which consistently show strong plus/minus despite low minutes. The LeBron-only lineups that do appear near the top pop more because of role-player balance than LeBron driving elite impact. The LeBron + Luka combinations sit in the middle — good, but not separating from the pack the way a superstar duo should. That suggests the Lakers still haven’t found a reliable identity when both are on the floor together.

Code 
lakers_tagged <- lakers |> 
  mutate(
    lineup_type = case_when(
      grepl("L. James", GROUP_NAME) & grepl("L. Dončić", GROUP_NAME) ~ "LeBron + Luka",
      grepl("L. James", GROUP_NAME) & !grepl("L. Dončić", GROUP_NAME) ~ "LeBron Only",
      !grepl("L. James", GROUP_NAME) & grepl("L. Dončić", GROUP_NAME) ~ "Luka Only",
      TRUE ~ "Other"
    )
  )
Code 
three_groups <- lakers_tagged |> 
  filter(lineup_type %in% c("LeBron Only", "Luka Only", "LeBron + Luka"))
Code 
three_groups <- three_groups |>
  mutate(
    MIN = as.numeric(MIN),
    PLUS_MINUS = as.numeric(PLUS_MINUS)
  )
Code 
library(ggplot2)

ggplot(three_groups, aes(x = MIN, y = PLUS_MINUS, color = lineup_type)) +
  geom_point(size = 3, alpha = 0.7) +
  theme_minimal(base_size = 14) +
  labs(
    title = "Luka minutes have carried the Lakers",
    x = "Minutes Played",
    y = "Plus/Minus",
    color = "Lineup Type"
  )

The chart shows that LeBron-only lineups aren’t producing positive value much, clustering around low minutes and neutral or negative plus/minus.

Luka-only lineups are carrying the team, with most of their minutes landing in positive territory. The LeBron + Luka lineups are just average, offering inconsistency instead of star-powered dominance.

Together, it suggests the Lakers still haven’t figured out how to mesh their two engines. And more quietly, it shows that LeBron isn’t able to lift flawed lineups the way he once could.
